# **Other Works by the Author**

The world functions because limits exist.

Limits of time. Of resources. Of information.

When those limits are stretched or disappear, systems stop behaving as expected.

The following works explore that same idea from different angles: power, structure, decision, and sustainability in contexts where traditional frameworks are no longer sufficient.

---

## **IRAN: The System That Challenges the World**

**Power, Strategy, and Global Influence**

For decades, Iran has been interpreted through headlines: crisis, sanctions, tension.

But that narrative does not explain how a system under constant pressure has managed to sustain itself, adapt, and project influence.

This book analyzes Iran not as a country, but as a strategic system.

A model that combines ideology, resilience, and geopolitical calculation to operate in a hostile environment without collapsing.

Beyond political positions, this work offers a structural perspective: understanding systems that do not follow traditional rules is key to understanding today’s global balance.

---

## **The Need for the New**

**A Story Transformation Engine**

What happens when we remove the limits that sustain reality?

This work presents a narrative system where every decision creates a new version of the world.

It is not about telling a linear story, but about constructing multiple realities and evaluating which ones can sustain themselves over time.

Eliminating death, altering time, or changing fundamental conditions generates consequences that affect the system’s balance.

It is an exploration of the constant need to redefine what is possible when limits stop being fixed.

---

## **Lethal Eternity**

What happens when time stops being a limit?

This work explores a scenario where life extends indefinitely.

What begins as progress becomes structural tension: decisions lose weight, systems saturate, and balance erodes.

Immortality does not eliminate the human problem.

It amplifies it.

Through a progressive narrative, the book shows how the absence of an end redefines the value of each action and questions whether a system can sustain itself when time no longer imposes limits.

---

## **Devil’s Throats**

Not all conflicts originate in ideology.

Some emerge at specific points in the world where resources, geography, and power intersect.

This work analyzes these strategic “chokepoints” where small variations generate global consequences.

More than a story, it is a study of structural pressure: how natural and human systems reach their limits when multiple forces converge in the same space.

The book reveals that global balance does not depend only on political decisions, but on critical points that, if altered, redefine the entire system.

---

## **Author’s Conceptual Line**

All works share a central question:

**What happens when systems exceed the limits they were designed for?**

Whether in geopolitics, narrative, artificial intelligence, or time itself, the approach remains the same:

to analyze structures, identify breaking points, and understand how they evolve when complexity demands something different.

This book on Mars and NUBELAI is part of that same line.

It is not an exception.

It is the natural continuation of an exploration into how systems change when they can no longer be sustained under their original conditions.

---

## **Closing**

Each work can be read independently.

But together, they reveal something broader:

a way of understanding the world not as isolated events,
but as systems in constant evolution.

And when seen from that perspective, many things stop feeling unexpected…

and begin to feel inevitable.
