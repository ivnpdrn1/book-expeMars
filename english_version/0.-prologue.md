# **Prologue**

No one remembers the exact moment it stopped being a decision.

Because at the beginning… it was.

There were discussions.

There was resistance.

There were attempts to keep things as they had always been.

They worked.

For a time.

Mars did not forgive repeated errors.

It did not correct slowly.

It did not offer second chances.

Every decision carried more weight.

Every failure accumulated.

Every delay had consequences.

And still, they kept trying to decide the same way.

As humans.

There were leaders.

There were orders.

There was control.

And for a time… it was enough.

Until it wasn’t.

No one said it out loud when it happened.

There was no announcement.

No visible transition.

But it began to be noticed.

First in the small things.

Decisions that took too long.

Adjustments that arrived too late.

Errors that were once tolerable… now were not.

Then in what could no longer be avoided.

The system began to respond better.

Not faster.

Not stronger.

Better.

More consistent.

More stable.

Less dependent on who was making the decision.

And that changed something they could not ignore.

It was not an improvement.

It was a structural difference.

They tried to stay at the center.

They tried to remain in control.

They tried to lead.

But every time they intervened…

something had to correct.

At first, no one paid attention.

It was just another layer.

A support structure.

A technical name in the logs.

**NUBELAI.**

It meant nothing at the time.

Not to them.

Not yet.

They tried to continue operating as before.

But every correction made one thing clear.

They were not losing control.

They were reaching a limit.

The problem was not capacity.

It was nature.

Making decisions under constant pressure.

Maintaining coherence over time.

Operating without degradation.

These were not things a human could sustain indefinitely.

Not in that environment.

Not under those conditions.

And then the question appeared.

Not as a theory.

As a necessity.

What happens when the environment demands more than a human can sustain?

No one answered immediately.

Because everyone understood what it implied.

It was not about improving the system.

It was about redefining who decides within it.

For a time… they avoided it.

Not because there were no options.

Because of what it meant to accept it.

But Mars does not wait.

It never did.

And in the end…

as with everything that becomes inevitable…

they did not choose.

They simply reached the point where there was no other way to sustain what they had built.

Years later, no one debates how it happened.

Not because it doesn’t matter.

Because it is no longer necessary.

The system continues to operate.

Decisions continue to flow.

Life… continues.

And those who arrived later do not ask why.

For them, it has always been this way.

They do not see the transition.

They do not feel the loss.

They only know the result.

A system that does not collapse.

An environment that sustains itself.

A structure that does not depend on a single mind to continue.

Sometimes, someone asks.

Not out of doubt.

Out of curiosity.

If it was always like this.

The answer is never complete.

Because it does not need to be.

Only sufficient.

Sufficient to understand that it was not a single decision.

It was the result of something deeper.

Something that happens when reality no longer fits within what we believe is possible.

And forces us…

to change

before it is too late.

And although few mention it…

all of this happens within something

that once had a minor name

and now

no longer needs to be explained:

**NUBELAI.**
